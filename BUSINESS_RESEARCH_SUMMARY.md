## 1. RESEARCH METHODOLOGY OVERVIEW

### Research Type
**Explanatory & Evaluative** — testing 5 business hypotheses using secondary quantitative data

### Methods Used
| Method | Application | Justification |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| **Secondary Market Analysis** | Market sizing, growth rates (12–15.5% CAGR) | Primary surveys resource-intensive; industry reports sufficient for opportunity validation |
| **Competitive Analysis** | Feature/pricing gaps, positioning white space | Direct competitor websites, published reviews, public pricing |
| **Regulatory Documentation Review** | CE/UKCA pathways, laser safety (EN 60825-1), GDPR | Standards publicly available; compliance costs from practitioner sources |
| **Cost Benchmarking** | BOM estimates, cloud infrastructure (AWS) | Component pricing from suppliers, published AWS pricing |
| **Triangulation** | Cross-reference 3+ sources for critical claims | Reduces single-source bias; increases confidence |

### Data Sources (Secondary Only)
- **Market Reports:** Global Market Insights, Research Nester, Market Data Forecast, Future Market Insights (6 firms, consensus 12–15.5% CAGR)
- **Competitor Intelligence:** Petcube, Furbo, ZUMIMALL, Enabot (direct website analysis + tech media reviews)
- **Regulatory:** EN 60825-1, EU CE Directives, UKCA guidance, GDPR/UK GDPR frameworks
- **Veterinary:** Published sources on laser play frustration (Catster, Animal Wellness Magazine, Buffington 2002)

### What Was NOT Done (Acknowledged Limitations)
- No primary consumer surveys (deferred to user testing phase)
- No prospective interviews (secondary to market opportunity validation)
- No systematic review protocol (PRISMA) — narrative review appropriate for MBA scope

### H2: Underserved Segment — PARTIALLY SUPPORTED ⚠️
**Finding:** No competitor combines: autonomous laser + adaptive AI + treat dispenser

| Competitor | Gap |
|------------|-----|
| Petcube Play 2 | Camera-first; laser is secondary; no AI adaptation |
| Furbo 360 | Dog-centric; no laser |
| ZUMIMALL | Random patterns; no learning |
| Enabot/Loona | General companion; not play-focused |

**Critical Uncertainty:** Treat dispenser mitigation for laser frustration — theoretically sound, **requires cat testing validation**

### H3: Subscription Viability — PARTIALLY SUPPORTED ⚠️
**Unit Economics (Base Case):**

| Metric | Year 1 | Year 3 | Assessment |
|--------|--------|--------|------------|
| Hardware COGS | €99.50 | €75 | Custom PCB transition |
| Hardware margin | €41.50 (27.7%) | €66.50 (44.3%) | Improving with scale |
| Subscription conversion | 50% trial-to-paid | 60% | Critical assumption |
| LTV:CAC (per buyer) | 1.4:1 | ~2.5:1 | Marginal → sustainable |
| LTV:CAC (per subscriber) | 4.4:1 | 5.0:1 | Strong when conversion achieved |

**Honest Assessment:** Year 1 economics marginal; sustainability depends on achieving 50% conversion + COGS reduction. Path to viability credible but carries execution risk.
