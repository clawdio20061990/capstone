## Methodology & Limitations

### Sampling Method
This survey uses **convenience sampling** through online cat owner communities (Facebook groups, Telegram channels). This is a non-probability sampling method.

### Limitations
1. **Self-selection bias:** Respondents who are highly engaged in cat owner communities may have different attitudes, spending patterns, and concern levels than the general cat-owning population. People who join cat communities are likely more invested in their cats' welfare, potentially inflating interest and willingness-to-pay metrics.
2. **Convenience sampling:** The sample is drawn from accessible online communities rather than randomly selected from the target population. This limits the ability to make statistical inferences about the broader market.
3. **Cannot extrapolate to general population:** Results should be interpreted as indicative of interest among engaged, digitally-active cat owners only. Findings cannot be generalized to all cat owners in the EU + UK market. Any market sizing based on these results should clearly note this limitation.
4. **Small sample size:** 50–100 responses allows identification of directional trends but is not adequate for statistical generalization. Descriptive statistics only — no complex inferential analysis is appropriate.

### Research Purpose
This survey is designed for MBA capstone project market validation at concept stage. Findings will inform product development priorities and pricing strategy but should be supplemented with additional research methods (prototype testing, competitive analysis, industry benchmarks) before making final go-to-market decisions.

## Prototype Testing: Friend & Family Feedback

**Participants:** 10–15 friends/family with cats  
**Duration:** 1–2 weeks with the device  
**Data Collection:** Informal chat/messenger feedback

### Feedback Questions (ask after 1 week)

1. Did your cat play with the device?
2. Did you notice any difference compared to a regular laser toy?
3. Would you recommend it to others? (Yes / No / Not sure)

### Optional
- Short video of cat playing (if they want to share)
- Any unexpected observations or issues

---

## Documentation for Capstone

The User Testing Report will include:

1. **Survey Results** — charts and summary of responses with sample size clearly indicated
2. **Prototype Feedback** — anonymized quotes from friends/family, general impressions
3. **Key Insights** — 3–5 bullet points of what we learned
4. **Limitations** — acknowledgment of self-selection bias, small sample size, and informal testing methodology
5. **Recommendations** — any product changes or validations based on feedback, with appropriate caveats about statistical significance

**Note:** This is lightweight, informal research suitable for an MBA capstone project at concept stage. No IRB approval required for non-medical consumer product research with voluntary adult participation and no collection of sensitive personal information. No compensation to participants. No complex statistical analysis — descriptive statistics only with clear acknowledgment of methodological limitations.
