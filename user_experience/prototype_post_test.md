## Researcher Notes & Limitations

### Methodological Limitations

**1. No Control Group:** This testing protocol does not include a control group (e.g., cats using a standard non-adaptive laser toy). Without a control, we cannot definitively attribute engagement differences to AI adaptation vs. novelty effect or other confounding factors. For an MBA capstone at concept stage, descriptive user feedback is sufficient; a controlled experiment would be required for academic publication or strong causal claims.

**2. Small Sample Size:** Prototype testing with a small number of households is not statistically representative. Results should be interpreted as preliminary feedback, not market validation.

**3. Self-Selection Bias:** Participants are self-selected (friends/family willing to help), not randomly sampled from the target market.

**4. Testing Duration:** Even 3 weeks may not fully capture long-term engagement patterns. Ideally, longitudinal testing over 2-3 months would be conducted, but this is not feasible for capstone timeline.

### Interpretation Guidelines

- **Positive feedback:** Suggests concept has potential; proceed with broader beta testing
- **Mixed feedback:** Identify specific issues to address in next prototype iteration
- **Negative feedback:** May indicate fundamental product-market fit issues; reassess concept or target segment

### Comparison with Pre-Test

**Key Metric:** Compare Question 4 (post-test daily playtime) with Pre-Test Question 6 (baseline daily playtime) to quantify behavior change. Note that this is self-reported and approximate, not measured with sensors.

**Qualitative Assessment:** Questions 5-6 provide subjective assessment of engagement and behavioral change.

### Next Steps

Results from this post-test, combined with survey data and market research, will inform:
1. Product refinement priorities
2. Beta testing protocol design
3. Marketing messaging (value proposition validation)
4. Pricing sensitivity assessment

**Important:** Do not overstate conclusions. This is formative research (concept testing), not summative validation (market proof). Language in capstone should reflect appropriate uncertainty: "early indications suggest..." not "research proves..."
