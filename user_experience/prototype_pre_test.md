**When to complete:** Before receiving the Reactacat device  
**Estimated time:** 4 minutes

## Informed Consent

**Study Purpose:** This is a product concept test for an MBA capstone project. We are evaluating a prototype autonomous cat toy to understand how cats interact with it and gather owner feedback.

**Participation:** Your participation is completely voluntary. You may stop participating at any time for any reason. You may also remove your cat from testing at any time if you have any concerns about their well-being.

**Data Use:** Your responses will be anonymized and used only for this research project. No personally identifiable information will be shared publicly. Video or photos you choose to share may be used in project documentation with your permission.

**Risks:** The device uses a Class 3R laser (5mW) with optical filter, designed to meet Class 1 safety conditions at typical play distances. The laser is similar to those in commercial pet toys. Treat dispensing is optional and you control treat types/amounts. Stop use immediately if you observe any signs of stress or unusual behavior in your cat.

## Researcher Notes

**Baseline Metric:** Question 6 provides a quantitative baseline for daily play time that can be compared with post-test results.

**Environmental Context:** Questions 8-10 help control for confounding variables (small apartment vs. large house may affect engagement; presence of other pets may affect behavior).

**Informed Consent:** Added at beginning per MBA capstone research ethics best practices, even though IRB approval is not required for non-medical consumer product testing with voluntary adult participation.
