RESEARCH PROPOSAL STRUCTURE
1. Research problem formulation
 * What managerial dilemma, decision problem, or organizational issue are you addressing?
 * What is/are your main research question(s)?
 * What is/are your hypothesis(es)? (If descriptive - use research questions instead; if relational - specify variables.)
 * Explain how your research question/hypotheses are:
   * Empirically testable / researchable
   * Feasible within the project scope and data access
   * Better justified than alternative explanations
2. Review of existing evidence
 * What does existing research and empirical evidence say about your problem?
 * Which type of literature review are you applying (narrative / critical / focused evidence review)?
 * What are the key gaps or unresolved issues identified in prior research?
 * What you already know and what you don't know?
 * How does existing evidence support your research questions and hypotheses?
3. Research design & methodological approach
 * What are the main research objectives and expected outcomes?
 * What is the overall research purpose?
   * Exploratory / Descriptive / Explanatory / Evaluation
 * Which research methods will you use?
   * Quantitative (e.g., survey, statistical analysis)
   * Qualitative (e.g., interviews, case study, FGIs)
   * Mixed methods (if applicable)
 * Why are these methods best fit-for-purpose for your problem?
 * How will the methods complement each other (triangulation)?
4. Data design & collection strategy
 * What data sources will you use?
   * Primary data (surveys, interviews, etc.)
   * Secondary data / desk research (reports, statistics, internal company data)
 * Why are these sources appropriate and sufficient?
 * What sampling approach will you use?
   * Quantitative sampling (probability / non-probability)
   * Qualitative sampling (purposive, snowball, etc.)
 * Why will this sampling strategy provide relevant and credible data?
 * How will data be collected (procedure, tools, access)?
 * What are potential practical constraints (access, time, resources)?
5. Data analysis & interpretation plan
 * What data analysis techniques will you use?
   * Quantitative: descriptive statistics, comparisons, correlations, etc.
   * Qualitative: thematic analysis, coding, content analysis, case analysis
 * Why are these techniques appropriate for your research questions?
 * How will you integrate quantitative and qualitative findings (if mixed methods)?
6. Reporting results & use in Capstone Project
 * How will you interpret results in relation to the problem?
 * How will research results support (or challenge) your project solution?
 * What will you do if findings contradict your expectations or planned solution?
